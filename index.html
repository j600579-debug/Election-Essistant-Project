📅 Key Dates

Election Timeline

Every election follows a structured calendar. Here's a typical general election timeline.

1
12–18 Months Before

Candidate Announcement

Political parties announce their candidates and campaigns begin. Candidates register with election authorities.

2
6–12 Months Before

Voter Registration Opens

Citizens can register to vote. Deadlines vary by region. Check your local election authority for exact dates.

3
3–6 Months Before

Primary Elections / Nominations

Parties hold primary elections or conventions to finalize their official candidates for the general election.

4
30–60 Days Before

Campaign Period

Active campaigning, debates, and public forums. Voters learn about candidates' policies and positions.

5
Election Day

Voting Day 🗳️

Registered voters go to their designated polling stations. Polls typically open early morning and close by evening.

6
Election Night & After

Vote Counting & Results

Ballots are counted and results are announced. Official certification may take days to weeks.
